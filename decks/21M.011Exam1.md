### Card
Q: Features of a chant

A:

- A genre (category of musical composition) with the following musical characteristics:
    - Monophonic texture
    - No fixed meter
    - No written accompaniment
    - Sacred
    - Melodies tend to step rather than leap

---

### Card
Q: Three types of chant

- **Syllabic (recitational)** – one note of music to one syllable of text
- **Neumatic** – several notes to one syllable
- **Melismatic** – many notes to one syllable


---

### Card
Q: Types of music texture

A:
- **Monophony** – a single melodic line without accompaniment. Chants are this
- **Homophony** – a melody with chordal accompaniment
- **Polyphony** – multiple independent melodic lines occurring simultaneously

Q: Estampie

A: A medieval dance form with a lively rhythm, often featuring repeated sections and instrumental accompaniment. It is one of the earliest known forms of instrumental music in Western history.

### Card

Q: Organum

A:
An early form of polyphony that developed from Gregorian chant, where a second melodic line is added to the original chant melody, often at a fixed interval such as a fourth or fifth.

Q: Motet

A:

Q: Lute song

A: A genre of secular vocal music that emerged in the Renaissance, characterized by a solo voice accompanied by a lute. Lute songs often feature poetic texts and intricate melodies.
